PromptEarn

From zero to $2M in revenue in 12 months

Challenge

Build an affiliate platform in a competitive market with no existing user base, limited engineering resources, and pressure to generate revenue fast.

Opportunity

Africa's affiliate marketing space was growing but underserved by well-built, reliable platforms. Founders needed payouts, tracking, and onboarding that actually worked.

Solution

Built the full platform as CTO and co-founder: affiliate tracking, payment flows, admin dashboards, email automation, and growth systems running in parallel with product development.

Execution

Owned engineering and growth simultaneously. Shipped weekly. Ran Meta campaigns, built landing pages, and iterated on conversion based on real user data.

Lessons learned

Revenue follows product clarity. The fastest growth came when the offer, onboarding, and payout experience were aligned. Engineering speed only matters when it's pointed at what users will pay for.

LeadThur

Building a global lead engine from scratch

Challenge

Sales teams and founders waste hours manually searching for business contact data. Existing tools were expensive, slow, or limited to specific regions.

Opportunity

A self-serve tool that returns 1,000+ verified business leads in 60 seconds, works in 195 countries, and monetizes through subscriptions and affiliates.

Solution

Built the entire product solo: scraping pipeline with Playwright, search UI, payment in naira and dollars, affiliate system, admin dashboard, and automated email flows.

Results

Live product serving users globally. Full ownership of infrastructure on Coolify VPS with Supabase, Paystack, and Flutterwave integrated end to end.

Lessons learned

Complex products need simple surfaces. Users don't care about the scraping pipeline. They care that it works fast, exports cleanly, and charges fairly.

Build Process

How I build products

A repeatable system for going from idea to live product with real users.

01

Research

Understanding users and validating demand before writing code. I talk to the market first, not after.

02

Product Strategy

Defining scope, positioning, and roadmap. What ships in v1, what waits, and what the business model looks like on day one.

03

Design

Creating user experiences focused on adoption. Mobile-first layouts, clear flows, and interfaces that don't need a tutorial.

04

Development

Building scalable production systems. Database design, payment integration, admin tools, and deployment from the start.

05

Launch

Shipping fast and collecting feedback. Real users on real infrastructure within weeks, not months of hidden development.

06

Growth

Iterating based on user behavior. Funnels, onboarding, retention, and the metrics that determine whether a product earns.
