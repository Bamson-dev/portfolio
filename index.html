About

I build products,
and I understand
what they need to earn.

Most developers are good at writing code, and that's where it ends for them. I got into building because I was a founder first — I needed things built, I saw how badly it could go when the builder didn't understand the business, and I decided to become the kind of person who could do both.

At PromptEarn, I was Co-Founder and CTO, I built the platform from the ground up, helped grow it to over 100,000 affiliates, and was responsible for every technical decision that affected whether the business worked. That experience changed how I think about every product I build — I'm always thinking about the user, the conversion, the payment flow, and what happens after someone signs up, not just whether the code runs.

The products below were all built to solve real problems for real people, some of them for clients, some of them as my own ventures, all of them live and working. That's the standard I hold every project to.

Three steps. That's it.

Step 1 — We have one real conversation

You tell me about the product, I ask the questions that help me understand the business behind it — who it's for, what it needs them to do, what success actually looks like in six months, not just at launch. One call is usually enough to know if we are a fit and roughly what it will take to build it properly.

Step 2 — I build it and keep you in the loop

I take full ownership of the build — design, frontend, backend, database, payments, deployment, all of it — so you are not coordinating between three different people who don't talk to each other. You get a clear update every single week, you can always reach me, and nothing about the progress is ever a mystery.

Step 3 — You launch with full ownership

When it goes live, everything is yours — the codebase, the servers, the documentation, the logins. No lock-in, no "only I can maintain this," no monthly retainer you never agreed to. If you want me to stay involved after launch to support growth or add features, that conversation is always available, but it is never forced.
